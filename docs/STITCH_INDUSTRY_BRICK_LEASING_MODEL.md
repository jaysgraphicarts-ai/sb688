# JGA Stitch Hive Mind — Industry Brick Leasing Model

## Purpose

The Stitch Hive Mind Operating System is designed to evolve into a leasable modular operating framework for different businesses and industries.

Each business or industry becomes a **Brick**.

Each Brick follows the Stitch Protocol, connects to the Hive Mind, and receives only the modules, memory, local AI tools, workflows, and mini-chip packages needed for that business domain.

The first connected business Brick is:

**Jay's Graphic Arts**

---

## Core Idea

The Stitch Hive Mind connects everything while keeping it pure.

The leasing model extends this principle to business operations:

```text
JGA Stitch Hive Mind OS
  ↓
Industry Brick Registry
  ↓
Business-Specific Brick
  ↓
Mini-Chip Modules
  ↓
Verified Workflows
  ↓
Memory + Activity + HR + Compliance + Local AI
```

Each business added to the system becomes an isolated, verified, evolving Brick.

No Brick becomes trusted until it passes Stitch verification.

---

## What Is a Business Brick?

A Business Brick is a modular operating unit inside the Stitch Hive Mind.

It contains:

- business identity;
- industry category;
- owner/tenant authority;
- allowed workflows;
- local AI policy;
- memory permissions;
- HR/session policy;
- upload/download rules;
- compliance requirements;
- automation modules;
- mini-chip packages;
- activity logs;
- verification gates;
- upgrade history.

Each Brick is leased, installed, configured, verified, and evolved under the Stitch Protocol.

---

## Mini-Chips

Mini-Chips are small modular capability packages.

They give a Brick the specific tools needed for its business or industry.

Examples:

- customer intake chip;
- invoice chip;
- quote generator chip;
- design proof chip;
- file upload chip;
- HR session chip;
- memory sync chip;
- local AI clarification chip;
- compliance chip;
- job tracker chip;
- content generator chip;
- recovery chip;
- audit chip;
- industry knowledge chip.

Mini-Chips must follow the same rule as all Stitch modules:

```text
No chip becomes trusted until verified.
```

## Stitch Protocol for Every Brick

Every Brick must follow this operating loop:

```text
register → classify → configure → verify → connect → operate → log → review → evolve
```

Every workflow inside a Brick must follow:

```text
receive → classify → clarify → purify → verify → act → log → store → harden
```


## Business Isolation

Each Business Brick must be isolated from other Business Bricks.

Rules:

1. One business cannot read another business's private memory without explicit owner approval.
2. Client files remain inside the correct Brick.
3. HR data is restricted to authorized roles.
4. Industry-specific knowledge can be shared only if classified as reusable and non-private.
5. Every cross-Brick connection must pass need-to-know review.
6. Every Brick has its own activity ledger.

---

## Evolution System

Each Brick evolves through verified upgrades.

Upgrade loop:

```text
need detected → chip proposed → sandbox tested → owner approved → installed → logged → monitored → hardened
```

The Hive Mind can recommend new chips when a business grows, but it cannot install sensitive or high-impact modules without owner approval.

## Truth Boundary

The leasing model is an operating architecture and software commercialization path.

It can support modular deployment, workflow automation, verification, memory, and local AI assistance.

It must not promise impossible guarantees.

No leased Brick may claim:

- perfect automation;
- no errors ever;
- unhackable security;
- legal/tax/medical certainty without qualified review;
- physical quantum computation;
- real consciousness transfer;
- deployment proof without runtime logs.

The system evolves by verified upgrades, not by unsupported claims.
